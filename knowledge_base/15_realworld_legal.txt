[TOPIC: LEGAL RIGHTS & INDIAN LAW]
[SOURCE: CONSTITUTION & IPC]

1. Police Interaction:
   - If stopped for traffic violation: You have the right to ask for their ID card.
   - A male police officer cannot arrest a woman between 6 PM and 6 AM (unless expert permission/lady constable present).
   - You can show Digilocker documents (DL, RC, Insurance); they are legally valid.

2. FIR (First Information Report):
   - Police cannot refuse to file an FIR for cognizable offenses (theft, assault).
   - If they refuse, send the complaint via registered post to the Superintendent of Police (SP).
   - Zero FIR: Can be filed in ANY police station, regardless of where the crime happened.

3. Cyber Crime:
   - If money is stolen online (UPI scam), call 1930 immediately (National Cyber Crime Helpline).
   - The 'Golden Hour' is the first 1-2 hours; chances of freezing money are highest.

4. Consumer Rights:
   - "No Refund/No Exchange" boards are illegal if the product is defective.
   - You cannot be charged more than MRP. Cooling charges for cold drinks are illegal.
   - Service Charge in restaurants is VOLUNTARY. You can ask to remove it.

5. Workplace Rights:
   - Equal Pay for Equal Work (Article 39d).
   - Maternity Leave: 26 weeks of paid leave is mandatory for companies with 10+ employees.
   - Sexual Harassment: Every company must have an Internal Complaints Committee (POSH Act).

6. Arrest Rights:
   - Police must tell you the grounds of arrest.
   - You have the right to a lawyer and a free phone call to family.
   - You must be presented before a magistrate within 24 hours.
